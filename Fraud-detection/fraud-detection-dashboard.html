Scam Playbook Analysis

HIGH THREAT

Structured behavioral analysis of modern scam typologies. Each playbook deconstructs the attacker's psychological manipulation tactics, phase-by-phase escalation patterns, and detectable risk signals — mapped against Cialdini's influence principles and behavioral science frameworks.

Romance Scam · SHA Tǔ Zhū Pán (杀猪盘) — Pig Butchering
01

Seeding & First Contact RECRUITMENT

Attacker initiates contact via dating apps, social media, or "wrong number" SMS. Profile is meticulously crafted: attractive photos (often stolen), high-status occupation (doctor, finance, engineer), international travel narrative. Initial messages are warm but non-threatening.

Profile too perfect Unsolicited contact Fast emotional escalation Never video calls
02

Love Bombing TRUST BUILDING

Rapid emotional bonding through daily contact, affectionate language, deep personal questions. Victim feels uniquely understood. Psychological mechanism: reciprocity + social proof. Duration: 2–8 weeks. Victim is gradually isolated from social support network through subtle criticism of friends/family.

Daily contact within days Calls you "soulmate" early Never meets in person Future faking (plans trips)
03

Financial Introduction GROOMING

Introduces crypto investment "casually" — mentions they recently made money, shows screenshots of returns. Frames as sharing a gift with someone they care about. First investment is small and always profitable (platform is controlled). Victim is rewarded to reinforce behavior.

Crypto investment mention Proprietary platform Guaranteed returns shown Small first deposit
04

Fattening the Pig ESCALATION

Victim is encouraged to deposit more after seeing "profits." Platform shows large unrealized gains. Scammer creates urgency (time-limited opportunity, exclusive access). Social proof used: "my uncle runs the platform, I got you special access." Victim may recruit friends/family.

Pressure to deposit more Urgency tactics Unrealized large profits Family/friends recruited
05

Withdrawal Block EXTRACTION

When victim tries to withdraw, platform invents fees: "tax prepayment," "verification deposit," "security unlock." Each fee is a new extraction. Emotional relationship is weaponized: scammer expresses disappointment if victim hesitates. Platform support is also controlled by same criminal network.

Withdrawal fees invented Tax / verification demands Emotional guilt used Support unresponsive
06

The Slaughter EXIT

Full disappearance or final extraction attempt. Account vanishes, platform goes offline. Average victim loss: $10,000–$150,000+. Emotional trauma compounded by shame, making victims reluctant to report. Criminal networks are organized, often based in Southeast Asia (Myanmar, Cambodia, Laos).

Sudden contact loss Platform offline Victim blames themselves Under-reported to authorities
Investment Fraud · Advance Fee / Ponzi Variants
01

Authority Establishment SETUP

Impersonates or claims affiliation with legitimate financial institutions, famous investors, or AI trading platforms. Uses professional-looking websites, fake Bloomberg/Forbes articles, fabricated license numbers. Cialdini trigger: Authority.

Unverifiable credentials Name-drops institutions Fake press coverage
02

FOMO Engineering MANIPULATION

Creates artificial scarcity and urgency. "Only 3 spots left," "IPO closes Friday," "This strategy is being shut down by regulators next month." Targets loss aversion — frames inaction as losing, not just missing out. Often uses fake countdown timers.

Countdown timers Limited spots narrative Regulatory threat framing
03

Social Proof Fabrication VALIDATION

Flood of fake testimonials, WhatsApp groups with bots showing fake wins, staged screenshots of bank transfers. "Proof" is pre-fabricated and visually convincing. Bots in group chats create false consensus. Victim is made to feel they would be foolish not to participate.

Screenshot-only proof Group chat with fake wins Cannot verify testimonials
04

Exit / Advance Fee EXTRACTION

Either disappears with funds (Ponzi collapse) or invents fees to release profits. Common fee names: "capital gains tax," "international wire release fee," "AML compliance deposit." Each payment is followed by another invented fee.

Fee to release profits AML fee demand Wire release fees
Authority Impersonation · Bank / Government / Tech Support
01

Fear Induction SHOCK

Spoofed caller ID from "your bank" or "police." Opens with alarming news: account compromised, warrant for arrest, illegal activity detected on your account. Creates immediate high-stress state. Under stress, critical thinking degrades — this is the attacker's goal. Victim is given no time to think.

Spoofed official number Immediate threat framing No time to think
02

Isolation & Control CAPTURE

"Do not tell anyone — this is a confidential investigation." Victim is instructed not to hang up, not to contact real bank. Creates dependency on attacker as the only trusted source. Phone held hostage for hours. Accomplices may play "supervisor" roles for added credibility.

Secrecy demand Do not contact bank Multi-hour call Supervisor escalation theater
03

Asset Transfer EXTRACTION

Instructs victim to move funds to "safe account" (controlled by attacker), buy gift cards and read codes, install remote access software, or withdraw cash for "evidence collection" by a "courier." Each method bypasses bank fraud controls.

Safe account transfer Gift card codes requested Remote access install Cash courier pickup
Fraud Intelligence Knowledge Base

Behavioral science frameworks and detection taxonomies underlying the Scam Detection Copilot's reasoning. Based on Cialdini's influence model, the BITE model of coercive control, and operational fraud intelligence experience.

Cialdini's 7 Principles

🧠

Scammers systematically exploit all 7 influence principles: Reciprocity (gifts/favors first), Commitment (small yes → big yes), Social Proof (fake testimonials), Authority (impersonation), Liking (relationship building), Scarcity (FOMO), Unity (in-group belonging).

Psychology Behavioral Science Detection Framework

BITE Model Signals

⚠️

Behavior, Information, Thought, Emotion control — originally developed for cult analysis, directly applicable to scam detection. Key signal: victim is systematically cut off from outside validation sources (friends, bank, search engine). Information monopoly is a critical red flag.

Coercive Control Isolation Detection BITE Model

Cognitive Hijacking

💡

Scammers deliberately induce emotional states (fear, love, excitement) that impair prefrontal cortex function. Decisions made under induced stress or romantic attachment bypass rational cost-benefit analysis. Interventions must address the emotional state first, facts second.

Neuroscience Emotional Manipulation Intervention Design

Network Analysis

🕸️

Fraud rings share infrastructure: IPs, device fingerprints, phone numbers, email domains, crypto wallets. Link analysis across merchant/transaction data reveals hidden connections. A single operator may run dozens of fake profiles simultaneously using the same device cluster.

Link Analysis OSINT Graph Intelligence

Pig Butchering Economics

📊

Industrial-scale operation. Criminal networks in SEA (Myanmar, Cambodia) operate call centers with trafficked workers. Average victim loss: $10k–$500k. Crypto rails used for irreversibility. FBI reports $3.5B+ in US crypto investment fraud losses in 2023 alone.

Threat Intelligence Crypto AML Organized Crime

Intervention Principles

🛡️

Effective intervention: (1) Non-accusatory — victim must not feel foolish, (2) Validate emotions first, (3) Ask questions rather than state facts, (4) Introduce doubt gradually, (5) Provide face-saving narrative. Direct confrontation causes psychological reactance and backfires.
